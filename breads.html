Sandwich Bread

Notes

  • 4 hrs
  • The dough needs a warm spot to rise. The porch works well.
  • Modified from Zero Waste Chef

Time & Serving

  • 30 minutes active
  • 3 hours resting
  • 30 minutes cooking
  • 4 hours total

30min active

Supplies

  • 1 cup liquid measuring cup
  • ½ measuring cup
  • tablespoon(2)
  • teaspoon
  • ¼ teaspoon

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of warm water
  • 2 tbs brown sugar or maple syrup or honey
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tbs olive oil or softened butter
  • 2¼ tsp active dry yeast
  • 2 cups + 2T wheat flour
  • 1½ cups unbleached bread flouror unbleached all-purpose

Directions

  1. Combine warm water, brown sugar, salt, oil, and yeast to large mixing bowl.
  2. Mix in whole wheat flour until well combined, on speed 1. Then add the bread flour ½ cup at a time. Add another few tablespoons of warm water, if dough is too dry
  3. Knead on speed 2 for about 7 minutes.
  4. Let rest for 1 to 1 ½ hours in a warm spot until doubled in size.
  5. Grease a loaf pan with canola oil. Punch down the dough. Turn out onto a floured surface. Flatten into a rectangle, approximately 8 inches by 6 inches. Beginning at a 6-inch side, roll up the dough and push in the sides gently, until you have formed an even log. Place the formed loaf in the pan, seam down, and tuck the ends under the loaves.
  6. Cover the loaves with the dishtowel. Let rest in a warm spot for about an hour until they puff up above the edge of the pan.
  7. About 10 minutes before the bread is ready to bake, preheat the oven to 425°F. Bake the bread for 28 30 minutes or until browned on top and the bottom sounds hollow when you tap it.
  8. Remove the loaves from the pans and cool completely on a wire rack before slicing.
  9. Store bread in a clean cloth bag in the fridge.
